about

    Rachel Neches leads data research at the Center for an Urban Future, an independent think tank in New York City. Her work focuses on making New York a better place for everyone, from Brooklyn to Buffalo.

    She graduated with a BA in Urban Studies (Economics) from Barnard College. Before joining the Center, she served as the Manager of Government Affairs and Research at the Times Square Alliance, where she leveraged data and advocacy to support the iconic neighborhood's recovery during the pandemic.

    Her research been referenced in numerous publications, including the New York Times, WNYC, Crain's New York Businesses, City Limits, New York Post, and more.
