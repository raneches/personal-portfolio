about

    Rachel Neches leads data research at the Center for an Urban Future, a New York City independent think tank. She writes on anything to do with making New York a better place, from Brooklyn to Buffalo.

    She graduated with a BA in Urban Studies (Economics) from Barnard College. Prior to joining the Center, Rachel served as the Manager of Government Affairs and Research at the Times Square Alliance, where she led advocacy efforts and evaluated Times Square's economic recovery from the pandemic.
    Her work has been referenced in numerous publications, including the New York Times, WNYC, Crain's New York Businesses, City Limits, New York Post, and more.
